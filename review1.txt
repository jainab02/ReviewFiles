Geography of India:
India occupies a significant portion of the Indian subcontinent and shares borders with several countries, including Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. It is surrounded by the Arabian Sea on the west, the Indian Ocean on the south, and the Bay of Bengal on the southeast. The country boasts a diverse topography, ranging from the snow-capped Himalayan mountains in the north to the expansive Thar Desert in the west, and from lush tropical rainforests in the south to the fertile Gangetic plains in the north.
